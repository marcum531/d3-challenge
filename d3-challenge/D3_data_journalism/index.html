Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

As poverty levels increase, the percentage of obesity increases, particularly in many of the southern states, such as Mississippi, West Virginia, Louisiana, and Arkansas. The percentage of people without healthcare also increases. This can also be seen in several of the southern states, such as Texas, Georgia, Louisiana, and Mississippi. The percentage of smokers increases as poverty levels increase. Again this can be seen in several of the southern states, including West Virginia and Kentucky.

As the median age increases, the percentage of people who are considered obese increases. This same trend can be seen in the percentage of smokers. The younger population tend to not smoke and maintain a healthy weight. Meanwhile, the younger population tend not to have healthcare.

As the median household income increases, the percentage of people without healthcare tends to decrease. This same trend can be seen with the percentage of smokers and the percentage of the population who are obese. Households with higher incomes generally are not smokers, maintaining a healthy weight, and can afford to have healthcare coverage.
